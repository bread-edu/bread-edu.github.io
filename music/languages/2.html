Music, in an abstract sense, is the duality of sound and silence.

Sound and silence form a duality of existence and nonexistence, of change and invariance, each the inverse of the other and each the definition of the other.

Sound is defined by silence, as sound requires silence to distinguish its ideas; silence is meaningful in its emptiness and absence, which allows for ponderance and interpretation.

Silence is defined by sound, as silence without sound is empty without meaning; sound is meaningful in its change and complexities, which allows for expression.

Each without its complement, either sound or silence eventually grows overwhelmingly repetitive and meaningless.
