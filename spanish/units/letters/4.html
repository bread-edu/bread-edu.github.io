Capitalization

Spanish letters can be uppercase (mayúscula) or lowercase (minúscula), just as in English.

In general, Spanish capitalization is infrequent compared to English.

If a word is capitalized in Spanish, its counterpart is also capitalized in English (the only exceptions are Sol and Luna for sun and moon), but the reverse is not necessarily true.

The following are capitalized in both Spanish and English:

  • The first letter of every sentence
  • The first letter of proper nouns
  • The common abbreviations of personal titles (and the full personal titles of Don and Doña)
  • The first letter of titles of books, movies, and other works
  • The first letter of every word in titles of newspapers and magazines
  • The names of religious festivals and national holidays

The following are capitalized not in Spanish, but in English:

  • Languages, nationalities, and geographical features
  • Words derived from the names of cities and countries
  • Full personal titles
  • Ordinal numbers following a name
  • Days of the week and months of the year
  • Religions and their adherents
