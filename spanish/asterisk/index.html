Let ∗ be the fundamental language for expression.

Expression (la expresión) is the manifestation of the universal human experience; human languages like music and Spanish are then merely partial aspects of ∗, expressed through experiences like hearing or sight.

Spanish (español) is an expressive language and collective identity.

As a human language, Spanish is expressed through speech and writing (el habla y la escritura) and learned through practice and immersion (la práctica y la inmersión).

As a collective identity, Spanish is the fourth most-spoken language globally, encompassing the expression of over half a billion speakers in over 25 countries.

The following material is intended for the process of learning Spanish from English, which is unique in its ease and interestingness due to the similarities between the two languages.

The following material will cover basic elements of Spanish (a common variant of United States Spanish), but not advanced elements, linguistic variants, or the literature, history, and culture of Spanish-speaking countries.
